Why Drone Bloom?

Many orchards use chemical thinning (e.g., Sevin) to manage bloom density. Drone Bloom investigates a robotics alternative—precise, data-driven thinning and mapping—to lower chemical load while sustaining yield and protecting pollinators.

Goals: 95% accurate stem thinning, high-precision tree geolocation, and practical workflows orchards can adopt.

Tech Focus

PX4/ArduPilot • MAVSDK-Python • OpenDroneMap/OpenSfM • OpenCV • GeoPandas • Open3D • ROS 2 + MoveIt (for arm)

Mapping

Photogrammetry (ODM/OpenSfM) → orthos/point clouds → canopy segmentation → tree centroids → GeoJSON.

Perception

OpenCV/torch for blossom & pedicel detection; RealSense/ZED depth; RTK/PPK for cm-level geotags.

Control

MAVSDK mission grids; ROS 2 nodes for arm servoing; safety interlocks; reproducible pipelines.
